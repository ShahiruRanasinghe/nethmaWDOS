The Department of Wildlife Conservation

The Department of Wildlife Conservation, established in 1949, is entrusted with preserving Sri Lanka's rich biodiversity. It manages a network of wildlife reservations covering over 13% of the country's land area, safeguarding water sources for rivers and 40 major reservoirs vital for agriculture and hydro-power. This network comprises reservations declared at different levels, and the department has sole responsibility for its oversight.
